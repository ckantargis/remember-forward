ABSTRACT (copy-paste this into the "Description" or "Abstract" field):
----------------------------------------------------------------------
An open-source system of laser-engraver-ready vector graphic (SVG) plate
designs for long-duration time capsules intended to preserve foundational
human knowledge and multiple writing systems through civilizational
disruption without reliance on electronic media, electricity, or
institutional continuity.

The system comprises two plate categories: (1) Knowledge plates (planned
series of 11) encoding survival skills, agriculture, technology re-founding,
governance principles, physics, energy generation, and encoding/communication;
and (2) Language plates (planned series of 200, four plates per language
across 50 languages) encoding script systems, phonology with IPA transcription,
grammar and core vocabulary, and interlinear-translated running text from
classical literature.

Key technical disclosures include:
- A standardized 480x680px portrait SVG format with double-border design
  optimized for nickel or stainless steel laser engraving at any scale
- A four-plate per language structure (Script / Phonology / Grammar / Text)
  with a standardized Bridge Phrase rendered in every language
- An interlinear translation methodology for Plate D (running text) providing
  word-for-word gloss, full translation, and source attribution
- A Kangxi radical indexing approach for the Mandarin Chinese plates
- A multi-tier container design specification (7 tiers, Tier 0 through Tier E)
  covering fired ceramic to lead-lined granite, with documented survivability
  estimates from 500 to 100,000+ years
- The bridge phrase: "This was made for you, freely, by people who remembered
  forward" — and its translation methodology for each target language,
  preserving idiomatic rather than mechanical equivalence

As of the filing date, 46 plates are complete (10 knowledge + 36 language
across 6 series: Hebrew [16 plates, 4 variants], Arabic, Tamil, Sanskrit,
Bengali, Mandarin Chinese). All designs are released under CC BY-SA 4.0.
